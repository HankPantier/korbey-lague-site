## The People Behind the Numbers

Most accounting firms introduce themselves in April and disappear by May. Korbey Lague PLLP works differently. Clients here work with the same people year-round — people who know their business structure, their goals for next quarter, and their name when they call. This is a firm built on real relationships, not a revolving door of seasonal preparers. Every person on this team is here because they make that model work.

<!-- block: content-split | variant: image-left | image: kelsey-korbey-cpa-office.jpg | query: "CPA partner reviewing client financials office" -->
## Kelsey Korbey, CPA — Founder & Managing CPA

Kelsey Korbey built this firm on a simple frustration: too many small business owners were getting tax returns and nothing else. A plan delivered in February, signed in April, and forgotten by June isn't advisory work — it's compliance. Kelsey set out to build something different in Tyngsborough.

A licensed CPA with deep roots in the Massachusetts market, Kelsey has spent her career working with business owners who needed more than a preparer — they needed someone who could read the numbers and tell them what to do next. That instinct shapes how the entire firm operates. The year-round advisory model, the tiered bookkeeping packages, the virtual CFO offering — all of it traces back to her conviction that clients deserve access to real financial thinking, not just at tax time.

Kelsey's client work spans closely held businesses, healthcare practices, and contractors — industries where cash flow timing, entity structure, and owner compensation decisions have real consequences. She brings the same directness to every engagement: here's what the numbers say, here's what it means, here's what we should do about it.

If you've ever wished your accountant would just tell you the truth and help you act on it, that's the practice Kelsey built.

## Our Advisory & Accounting Staff

The team at Korbey Lague brings credentials that matter — CPAs, MBAs, and a Personal Financial Specialist on staff — plus real-world experience in the industries clients actually work in.

### Ron Lague, CPA, PFS
Ron holds both a CPA license and the Personal Financial Specialist (PFS) credential — an AICPA designation awarded only to CPAs who demonstrate advanced proficiency in personal financial planning. That combination matters for clients whose business finances and personal finances are tightly linked: contractors managing equipment debt and retirement accounts, practice owners weighing buy-sell agreements, or business owners planning an exit. Ron brings tax strategy and financial planning into the same conversation, so nothing falls through the gap between the two.

### Jackie Estes, MBA
Jackie's MBA gives her a strong grounding in business operations — she approaches client work at the intersection of accounting and strategy. She's particularly effective with clients who are scaling, restructuring, or trying to make sense of financials that have gotten ahead of their management systems. Jackie is often the person clients hear from when a question goes beyond the numbers on the page.

### Mike Riordan, MBA
Mike's background combines accounting discipline with the broader business perspective his MBA brings to client engagements. He's built strong working knowledge in areas where financial decisions have operational consequences — including contractor businesses and project-based work. Clients describe him as someone who explains things clearly without talking down to them.

### Richard DelGaudio, CPA
Richard is a licensed CPA with experience across tax compliance, financial reporting, and small business advisory work. He's precise where precision is required and practical where it isn't — a balance that serves clients well during both tax season and the quieter months when planning work happens. His presence on the team means clients have another credentialed set of eyes on anything that matters.

## How We Work With You

At Korbey Lague, clients don't get passed around. When you come on as a client, you have a consistent point of contact — someone who knows your file, your history, and what you're working toward. That person coordinates with the rest of the team as needed, but the relationship stays consistent.

Communication doesn't stop on April 16th. Clients receive proactive outreach when tax law changes affect their situation, when quarterly estimates are due, and when something in their financials warrants a conversation. The team uses Sage Intacct for clients on bookkeeping and virtual CFO engagements, which means real-time visibility into financials — not a monthly PDF dropped in an inbox.

If you have a question in July, someone here will answer it.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Our Team

**Q: Who will I actually work with at Korbey Lague PLLP?**
A: You'll have a consistent point of contact from the start — someone who knows your business, your history, and your goals. The team coordinates internally as needed, but the relationship doesn't get handed off. You won't be explaining your situation from scratch every time you call.

**Q: What credentials does the Korbey Lague team hold?**
A: The team includes multiple licensed CPAs, two MBAs, and a Personal Financial Specialist (PFS) — an AICPA credential held by Ron Lague that combines CPA expertise with advanced personal financial planning. That depth of credentialing means most client questions can be handled in-house without referrals elsewhere.

**Q: What is a Personal Financial Specialist (PFS)?**
A: The PFS is an advanced credential awarded by the AICPA exclusively to licensed CPAs who meet rigorous requirements in personal financial planning. Ron Lague holds this designation, which allows him to integrate tax planning and personal financial strategy in a single engagement — especially valuable for business owners whose personal and business finances overlap.

**Q: Does Korbey Lague work with clients outside of tax season?**
A: Yes — that's the firm's core model. Year-round advisory services include proactive tax planning, quarterly estimate management, bookkeeping, and virtual CFO work. Clients receive outreach when their situation warrants it, not just when a filing deadline is approaching.
