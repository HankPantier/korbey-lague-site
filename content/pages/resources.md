## Financial Guides by Industry

Generic accounting advice is easy to find. We built these guides because each industry carries its own financial pressure points — and a one-size answer usually fits nobody.

### Healthcare Professionals
icon: Stethoscope

Practice owners face billing complexity, payroll for clinical staff, and entity structure decisions that carry long-term tax consequences. This guide covers the financial benchmarks and planning moves most relevant to physicians, dentists, and allied health providers in Massachusetts.

### Contractors and Trades
icon: Hammer

Job costing, equipment financing, bonding requirements, and cash flow gaps between draws — these are not generic small business problems. This guide speaks directly to the financial reality of running a construction or trade business.

### Nonprofits
icon: HeartPulse

IRS compliance, Form 990 strategy, grant tracking, and board-level financial reporting all come with their own rules. This guide helps nonprofit leaders understand what strong financial management looks like — and what auditors look for.

### Service-Based Businesses
icon: Briefcase

From consulting firms to agencies to professional services, this guide addresses owner compensation strategy, quarterly tax exposure, and when your bookkeeping setup is holding your growth back.

### Startups
icon: Lightbulb

Early-stage decisions about entity structure, accounting software, and payroll have consequences that show up years later. This guide helps founders avoid the mistakes that are expensive to unwind — before they make them.

## Tax Planning Checklists and Deadline Calendars

Tax planning isn't a fourth-quarter activity. The moves that actually reduce your liability happen throughout the year — in June when you're deciding whether to buy equipment, in September when estimated payments are due, in November when there's still time to accelerate deductions or defer income. These tools keep you ahead of those windows, not scrambling after them.

**What's available:**

- Quarterly estimated tax payment calendar (with Massachusetts-specific deadlines)
- Year-end tax planning checklist for business owners
- Self-employed and S-Corp owner compensation review checklist
- Business entity comparison reference guide
- Payroll tax deposit schedule for small employers
- Retirement contribution deadline tracker

These aren't auto-generated templates. They reflect the actual planning calendar Ron Lague, CPA, PFS and the team at Korbey Lague PLLP use with clients throughout the year. Download them, put them in your calendar, and use them — or better yet, bring them to a consultation and we'll walk through them together.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Resources

**Q: Do I need to be a current client to use these resources?**
A: No. The guides, checklists, and articles on this page are available to anyone. They're built to be genuinely useful on their own. That said, many people find that working through a checklist raises questions that are worth bringing to a consultation — and we're happy to schedule one whether you're an existing client or not.

**Q: How often does Korbey Lague update its articles and guides?**
A: The team publishes new articles and refreshes guides on a rolling basis throughout the year — not just in tax season. When the IRS updates thresholds, when Massachusetts changes a rule, or when a recurring client question signals a gap, that's when new content goes up. Ron Lague, CPA, PFS and Kelsey Korbey, CPA are primary contributors.

**Q: What's the difference between working with a CPA year-round versus just at tax time?**
A: Year-round advisory means the firm is involved in the decisions that shape your tax return — not just in filing it. That includes estimated payment planning, entity structure reviews, bookkeeping oversight, and CFO-level analysis through Sage Intacct. Filing is the outcome; planning is the service. The checklists and calendars on this page are a small preview of that approach.

**Q: Which industries does Korbey Lague PLLP specialize in?**
A: The firm has specific depth in healthcare practices, contractors and trades, nonprofits, service-based businesses, and startups. The industry guides on this page reflect real planning scenarios the team handles regularly — not generic templates repurposed from a national library. Each niche carries different tax structures, compliance requirements, and cash flow patterns.

**Q: How do I get started if I have questions after reading a guide or checklist?**
A: The fastest path is to schedule a consultation directly through the website at /contact. Initial meetings are structured around your specific situation — industry, entity type, current pain points — not a sales script.
