## We Stand Between You and the IRS

An IRS audit notice lands in your mailbox and the first thing most people feel is dread. What did I miss? How bad is this? Do I need a lawyer? The questions pile up fast, and the IRS isn't going to wait while you figure out the answers.

Korbey Lague PLLP handles audit representation for individuals and businesses throughout Massachusetts — and we do it year-round, not just when tax season wraps up. When you work with us, you don't face the IRS alone. You don't speak to them directly, you don't sort through years of records by yourself, and you don't guess at what the examiner is actually looking for. That's our job.

Receiving an audit notice doesn't mean you did something wrong. It means you need a CPA in your corner who knows exactly how this process works — and who has the credentials and the experience to represent you effectively from the first letter to the final resolution.

## What Audit Representation Actually Means

There's a meaningful difference between filing your taxes and representing you when those taxes are questioned. Tax preparation is compliance. Audit representation is advocacy.

As licensed CPAs, the credentialed professionals at Korbey Lague PLLP are authorized to represent taxpayers before the IRS — at every level, including examination, appeals, and collection. That means we communicate directly with the IRS on your behalf. You don't sit in a room with an examiner. You don't field calls from IRS agents. You don't try to decipher technical correspondence that's designed to be confusing.

Ron Lague, CPA, PFS, and our team of CPAs hold designations through the AICPA — credentials that require demonstrated competency in tax and financial advisory, not just familiarity with tax software. When we represent you, the IRS knows it's dealing with professionals who understand the rules as well as they do. That changes the dynamic significantly.

## Types of Audits We Handle

Not every audit looks the same. The IRS uses several different examination formats depending on what triggered the review and how complex your situation is. Here's what each one means — and what to expect.

### Correspondence Audit
icon: FileText

The most common type. The IRS sends a letter requesting documentation to support a specific item on your return — a deduction, a credit, or reported income. These look routine, but responding incorrectly or incompletely can escalate the issue. We handle the response from start to finish.

### Office Audit
icon: Building

You're asked to bring records to a local IRS office for review. An agent will examine specific items in person. We attend in your place — or alongside you — and manage every part of the conversation with the examiner.

### Field Audit
icon: ClipboardCheck

An IRS agent comes to your home or business to conduct the examination on-site. Field audits are typically reserved for more complex returns or businesses. Having a CPA present to control what the agent sees — and how questions are answered — is critical.

### IRS Notices and CP Letters
icon: Receipt

Not every IRS letter is a formal audit, but every IRS letter demands a response. CP2000 notices, balance-due notices, and penalty letters each require different handling. Ignoring them or responding without guidance is one of the most common — and costly — mistakes taxpayers make.

### State Tax Audits
icon: MapPin

Massachusetts DOR audits require the same level of focused representation as IRS examinations. State examiners follow different procedures and rules than their federal counterparts, and experience with both matters.

### Payroll Tax Examinations
icon: Coins

IRS payroll audits are among the most aggressive. Worker classification, 941 discrepancies, and Trust Fund Recovery Penalties can result in significant personal liability for business owners. This is not the area to handle without experienced representation.

## Who We Typically Represent

Certain taxpayers face higher audit risk — not because they've done anything wrong, but because of how their income is structured, how their deductions look on paper, or the industry they're in. Korbey Lague PLLP has concentrated experience in exactly these groups.

### Healthcare Professionals
icon: Stethoscope

Practice owners, physicians, and dentists often carry large deductible expenses, complex entity structures, and revenue patterns that can draw IRS scrutiny. Industry-specific knowledge means we understand what's defensible — and how to document it.

### Contractors and Trades
icon: Hammer

Worker classification, vehicle and equipment deductions, and home office claims are all common audit triggers for contractors. Examiners look hard at these categories. We know what documentation holds up and how to present it.

### Business Owners
icon: Briefcase

Schedule C and pass-through business income is consistently among the highest-audited categories in the tax code. If your business return is flagged, the audit can extend to related personal returns. We represent both — together.

### Nonprofits and Tax-Exempt Organizations
icon: ShieldCheck

Form 990 examinations and unrelated business income reviews require specialized knowledge of exempt organization rules. We understand the compliance requirements that apply to Massachusetts nonprofits and can represent your organization throughout the process.

## Our Audit Representation Process

Audit representation isn't a single phone call. It's a structured process, and transparency at every stage makes a real difference. Here's how we work through it.

### Review the Notice
We start by examining exactly what the IRS or state agency is asking for — the specific tax year, the items under examination, and the response deadline. Understanding the scope of the audit before responding is non-negotiable.

### Gather and Organize Documentation
We identify every document that supports your return, request what we need from you in plain language, and organize the records in the format examiners expect. Handing over a disorganized stack of receipts is not a strategy.

### Develop a Representation Plan
Before any communication goes to the IRS, we build a clear position. If the examiner has a legitimate question, we answer it precisely. If they've made an error or applied a rule incorrectly, we challenge it with documentation and authority — not frustration.

### Communicate Directly with the IRS
All correspondence, calls, and meetings with the IRS or state agency go through us. You are kept informed at every step, but you are not in the middle of the conversation.

### Negotiate and Resolve
Most audits end in one of three outcomes: full acceptance, a partial adjustment, or an appeal. We pursue the best result the facts support — and if an assessment is wrong, we file the appeals necessary to correct it. Our year-round availability means there are no gaps in representation because tax season ended.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Audit Representation

**Q: Do I have to meet with the IRS myself if I hire Korbey Lague PLLP for audit representation?**
A: No. As licensed CPAs, the professionals at Korbey Lague PLLP are authorized to represent you before the IRS at the examination level and beyond. We communicate directly with IRS agents and examiners on your behalf. In most cases, you will have no direct contact with the IRS during the audit process.

**Q: What is the difference between a correspondence audit and a field audit?**
A: A correspondence audit is conducted by mail — the IRS requests specific documents to support an item on your return. A field audit involves an IRS agent visiting your home or business to conduct a broader in-person review. Field audits are more intensive, but both types require the same level of careful documentation and professional response.

**Q: How quickly do I need to respond to an IRS audit notice?**
A: IRS notices include firm response deadlines — typically 30 to 90 days depending on the notice type. Missing a deadline can limit your options and may result in automatic assessments or expanded examination scope. Contact Korbey Lague PLLP as soon as you receive any IRS or Massachusetts DOR correspondence.

**Q: Can Korbey Lague PLLP represent me if I used a different accountant to file my taxes?**
A: Yes. You are not required to use the firm that prepared your return for audit representation. Korbey Lague PLLP can review your filed return, gather the necessary documentation, and represent you before the IRS regardless of who prepared the original filing.

**Q: Does Korbey Lague PLLP handle Massachusetts state tax audits in addition to IRS audits?**
A: Yes. The firm provides representation before the Massachusetts Department of Revenue as well as the IRS. State audits follow different procedures and apply state-specific rules — experience with both federal and Massachusetts state examinations is essential for complete representation.
