## Common Business Tax Mistakes We Help You Avoid

These aren't hypothetical errors. They're the problems we see most often when a new client comes to us after working with a seasonal or generalist preparer.

- **Wrong entity structure for the business's size and income.** An LLC taxed as a sole proprietor might have made sense at $80K in revenue. At $300K, it's likely costing the owner thousands in self-employment tax annually.
- **Missed deductions that require planning, not just paperwork.** Home office, vehicle use, retirement contributions, and Section 179 elections all require documentation and intentional decisions — not just a box checked at filing time.
- **Estimated tax penalties from poor quarterly planning.** If your income fluctuates and your estimates don't adjust, the IRS penalty adds up fast. This is preventable with proper mid-year review.
- **Payroll tax misclassification.** Treating employees as contractors — or vice versa — triggers audits and back liabilities that dwarf the original tax savings.
- **Reactive-only planning.** Waiting until March to review the prior year means every decision is already locked. The businesses that pay the least in tax are the ones where planning conversations happen in Q3 and Q4, while there's still time to act.
- **Overlooking multi-state obligations.** One remote employee in another state can create nexus you're not aware of — and failing to file creates back-year exposure.

These aren't mistakes Korbey Lague fixes after the fact. The structure of our client relationships is designed to prevent them in the first place.
