Most business owners see their CPA once a year — usually in a scramble before April 15. The return gets filed, the bill gets paid, and the relationship goes quiet until next January. That's compliance. It's necessary. But it's not strategy.

Korbey Lague PLLP was built on a different premise: that the decisions shaping your business happen in July, not April. When you're weighing a new hire, negotiating a lease, or wondering why cash feels tight despite strong revenue — that's when you need a financial voice in the room.

A Virtual CFO gives you exactly that. CFO-level insight, financial discipline, and strategic clarity — without the cost of a full-time executive. It's the service that turns your CPA relationship from a once-a-year obligation into a year-round advantage.

The title sounds impressive. Here's what it means in practice.

A Virtual CFO from Korbey Lague PLLP works alongside you to build the financial infrastructure your business needs to grow with intention — not just react to whatever the month throws at you. That means cash flow forecasting so you can see problems before they become crises. It means budgeting that reflects your actual business model, not a generic spreadsheet template. On the reporting side, you'll receive financial statements that are readable and explained — not just delivered as a PDF attachment and left for you to interpret. Korbey Lague uses Sage Intacct to provide clients with real-time financial visibility, which means your data is current when it counts.

Strategic planning is where this service earns its keep. Whether you're evaluating an acquisition, preparing for a bank loan, modeling the cost of a new service line, or planning an ownership transition, a Virtual CFO brings the financial rigor to stress-test decisions before you make them. The goal is straightforward: fewer surprises, better decisions, a clearer picture of where your business is headed.

## Signs You've Outgrown Tax-Only Support

Some business inflection points are obvious. Others quietly signal that the annual CPA call is no longer enough.

You've outgrown tax-only support if:

- Cash flow feels unpredictable — revenue is up but you're still waiting on money
- A major decision is coming — a new hire, a location, an equipment purchase — and you don't have a financial model to back it up
- A lender or investor is asking for projections, forecasts, or multi-year financials
- You're not sure which of your services or clients is actually profitable
- Growth is happening faster than your systems can track it
- You've received a tax bill that surprised you — because no one was looking ahead
- You have a bookkeeper but no one is interpreting what the numbers mean
- You've written the phrase "I'll figure it out later" about something financial

If more than two of these land, the conversation is worth having.
